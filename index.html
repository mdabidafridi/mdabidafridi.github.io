About Kaiser Hamid

Kaiser Hamid is currently pursuing a Ph.D. at Texas Tech University, where he is serving as a Graduate Research Associate in the Human-in-the-loop Advanced Cognitive Engineering Research Lab, under the guidance of Dr. Nade Liang.

His research is focused on Human-Computer Interaction and Computer Vision. A system is being developed that facilitates seamless interaction between humans and autonomous vehicles, leveraging computer vision, deep learning, and insights from human factors research to ensure safe, intuitive, and user-centered experiences on the road.

Previous research by Kaiser explored the domains of Travel Behavior and Passive Data, under the guidance of Professors Dr. Annesha Enam and Dr. Samiul Hasan. The potential applications of Google Location History data as an origin-destination (O-D) matrix were examined, revealing valuable observations about travel patterns and behavior. During his undergraduate thesis in the domain of Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), under the mentorship of Prof. Dr. Moazzem Hossain, Kaiser investigated queue length estimation using computer vision techniques, aimed at improving traffic management strategies and enhancing transportation efficiency.

Outside of academic pursuits, outdoor activities are enjoyed by Kaiser, with a dream to visit many countries. Time is also spent at the gym to stay fit, and he enjoys hanging out with friends and family, always excited to try new types of food.
